Discover Your Diamond

Explore the five essential characteristics that define a diamond's beauty and value

Round Brilliant

Select Your Diamond Shape

Each shape offers unique brilliance and character. Choose the form that speaks to your style.

Expert Insight

Round brilliants are the most sought-after shape, representing 60% of all diamond purchases. Fancy shapes like oval and pear can appear up to 10% larger for the same carat weight.

Carat Weight

The weight of the diamond measured in carats. One carat equals 200 milligrams. Larger diamonds are exponentially rarer and more valuable.

Expert Tip

Consider diamonds just below popular carat weights (0.90 ct vs 1.00 ct) for better value without visible size difference.

1.00 Carat

Color Grade

Diamond color is graded from D (completely colorless) to M (light yellow or brown). The less color, the higher the grade and value.

Faint Yellow Near Colorless Colorless
Expert Tip

G-H color diamonds offer excellent value with no visible color to the untrained eye. D-F are premium colorless grades.

E - Colorless

Clarity Grade

Clarity measures internal and external imperfections called inclusions and blemishes. Fewer and smaller inclusions mean higher clarity and value.

Expert Tip

VS2 and SI1 clarity diamonds are eye-clean (no visible inclusions) and offer the best value for most buyers.

SI1 - Slightly Included

Cut Quality

Cut determines how well a diamond reflects light. Better cuts produce more brilliance, fire, and scintillation - the diamond's sparkle.

Poor Cut - Light Escapes
Excellent Cut - Light Returns
Expert Tip

Cut is the most important factor for a diamond's beauty. An Excellent or Ideal cut grade ensures maximum brilliance and sparkle.

Very Good Cut - Excellent Light Return

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about finding your perfect diamond

While all 4Cs are important, Cut is often considered the most crucial as it directly affects the diamond's brilliance and sparkle. A well-cut diamond will appear more brilliant than a poorly cut diamond of higher color or clarity. However, the "most important" factor ultimately depends on your personal preferences and priorities.

The right carat size depends on your budget, finger size, and personal preference. Consider that a well-cut smaller diamond can appear larger and more brilliant than a poorly cut larger stone. Also, certain settings can make diamonds appear larger. We recommend trying different sizes to see what looks best and feels comfortable.

For white diamonds, grades D-F are colorless, G-J are near-colorless, and K-M show faint color. Most people cannot distinguish between D-G grades with the naked eye. G-H grades offer excellent value with no visible color in most settings. The metal choice also affects appearance - yellow gold can mask slight color better than platinum or white gold.

Certification from reputable labs like GIA, IGI, or AGS provides an independent, objective assessment of a diamond's characteristics. It ensures you're getting exactly what you're paying for and makes comparison shopping easier. We strongly recommend purchasing only certified diamonds for transparency and peace of mind.
